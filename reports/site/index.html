AI Capstone · Do AI Adoption Signals Predict Company Performance? · July 2026

Specificity is the signal.

We measured AI adoption across ~500 large U.S. companies — scores, filings, and 30,861 classified job postings — and tested it against real financial outcomes. What predicts growth is not how loudly a company talks about AI, but how concretely.

01The Finding

AI adoption is visible in fundamentals — if you measure deeply enough.

Every evidence-based adoption signal we tested — Larridin's Tracker scores and the dimensions we built to extend them — is associated with faster revenue growth. The phenomenon is real. But the signals separate sharply once you demand more of them.

Narrative concreteness — whether a company's own 10-K reports named, deployed AI use cases with quantified results, rather than aspirational language — survives every control we impose: sector, firm size, and prior growth momentum. Moving from the least to the most concrete discloser is worth +8.7 points of year-over-year revenue growth (p = 0.007). Composite scores capture the phenomenon; evidence-anchored depth is what sharpens it into signal.

Meanwhile, none of these public signals predicts stock returns — the market has already priced them — and margins show no effect yet. AI, today, is a growth story, not a cost story.

04Method & Validation

Built evidence-first. Stress-tested before we believed it.

Signals enter as cross-sectional percentile ranks; regressions use HC3 robust errors, 1%/99% winsorization, and Benjamini–Hochberg false-discovery control. Every LLM-extracted score must cite verbatim source evidence. The design is a single cross-section — associations, not causal claims.
